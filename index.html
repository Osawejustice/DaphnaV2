This FAQ section addresses common questions regarding private nursing and home care services, it covers essential topics

We specialize in caring for children and teens with developmental disabilities such as Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Global Developmental Delay, Down Syndrome, speech and language delays, intellectual disabilities, and others.

Shared residential living refers to homes with two to four people living together, with professional support provided by Daphna caregivers and nurses

Daphna offers both personal care and medical support in the home. Our services include assistance with daily living tasks such as bathing, grooming, feeding, dressing, toileting, mobility and transfers

It starts with a complimentary evaluation. We meet with you (and/or your loved one and family) to review the care needs, discuss preferences and schedules, and address specific medical or support requirements. From there, we create a customized care plan that reflects what was discussed. We also match caregivers who are compatible and provide you with information about ongoing communication, oversight, and how to request changes as needs evolve

Daphna understands that navigating funding, application forms, and government or insurance paperwork can be overwhelming. We offer assistance with filling out forms related to funding programs, grants, or other supports (like AISH in Alberta). We can guide you through the process so you don’t have to go it alone
